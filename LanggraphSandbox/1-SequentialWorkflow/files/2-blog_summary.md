Exoplanet discovery has transformed the ancient "are we alone?" question into empirical science, revealing thousands of diverse worlds beyond our solar system. Ingenious detection methods—including radial velocity, transit photometry, direct imaging, microlensing, and astrometry—have unveiled a "zoo" of planetary types, from "Hot Jupiters" to "Super-Earths," enabling atmospheric characterization for key molecules. This research, driven by missions like Kepler, TESS, and JWST, seeks habitable zones and biosignatures, fundamentally reshaping our cosmic perspective and suggesting a universe teeming with possibilities for life and diverse planetary architectures.
