Exoplanet research has rapidly evolved from speculation to a robust scientific field, confirming thousands of planets beyond our solar system and fundamentally reshaping our understanding of planetary formation and diversity. Scientists employ ingenious indirect methods like transit photometry and radial velocity, alongside direct imaging and gravitational microlensing, to uncover a vast menagerie of worlds, from scorching Hot Jupiters to potentially habitable Super-Earths and rogue planets. This pursuit centers on identifying planets within habitable zones and detecting atmospheric biosignatures using advanced telescopes like JWST, which is revolutionizing our ability to characterize exoplanet atmospheres. This ongoing quest promises to fundamentally shift humanity's perception of its place in a diverse, potentially populated cosmos.
